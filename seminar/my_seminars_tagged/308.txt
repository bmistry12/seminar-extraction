Delaying the use of a knowledge source during language comprehension introduces computational inefficiency in the form of overgeneration . On the other hand , ensuring that all relevant knowledge is brought to bear as each word in the sentence is understood is a difficult design problem . As a solution to this problem , we present NL-Soar , a language comprehension system that integrates disparate knowledge sources automatically . Through experience , the nature of the understanding process changes from deliberate , sequential problem solving to recognitional comprehension that applies all the relevant knowledge sources simultaneously to each word .
